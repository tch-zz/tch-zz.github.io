    • Nom nom

    Overview

    Nom Nom is a self-initiated casual merge game inspired by local street food culture and night market business operations. The game is designed to provide a relaxing and accessible experience, allowing players of all ages including elderly players, to enjoy low-pressure, rewarding gameplay while learning about traditional food culture.

    Design Goal

    The project aimed to create a culturally immersive yet easy-to-understand game experience that:
    ☻ Introduces local street food culture through interactive gameplay
    ☻ Provides stress-free and accessible mechanics suitable for casual players
    ☻ Combines collection, progression, and simulation systems to maintain long-term engagement
    ☻ Encourages player interaction through community-driven content

    Gameplay Loop

    Players begin with an initial fund provided by the game, which they use to establish and operate their own night market food stand. The main gameplay cycle includes:
    ☻ Building and customizing their food stand
    ☻ Managing monthly night market rental costs
    ☻ Producing and upgrading food items through merging
    ☻ Completing stages to earn experience points (EXP) and in-game currency
    ☻ Expanding business operations through upgrades and new unlockable content

    Design Concept

    All visual components and gameplay systems are inspired by authentic street food culture and night market environments. The game simulates the experience of operating a local food stand, allowing players to engage with cultural storytelling while managing a small business. The design emphasizes warmth, familiarity, and cultural authenticity to create an engaging and educational player experience. By combining merge mechanics with business simulation, the game encourages exploration, collection, and long-term progression.

    UX Design Considerations

    ☻ Simplified mechanics to ensure accessibility for a wide age range
    ☻ Clear visual feedback during merge interactions to enhance player satisfaction
    ☻ Gradual introduction of complexity to avoid overwhelming new players
    ☻ Cultural storytelling integrated into progression systems to increase emotional engagement
    ☻ Community participation features to encourage player retention and interaction

    CRM system for hairdressers

    Hairdressers relied on handwritten records to document customer services, hair conditions, and treatment details. While familiar, the process caused slow documentation, calculation errors, and longer customer waiting times. The project aimed to transform the workflow into a digital system that improves efficiency without disrupting existing habits.

    Device

    iPad, tablets

    Goal

    Digitise paper-based customer records while preserving hairstylists’ natural workflow.

    Problem

    Hairdressers documented every service manually on paper sheets:
    ☹︎ Time-consuming documentation during busy service hours
    ☹︎ Manual price calculation increased risk of error
    ☹︎ Difficult to retrieve historical customer records quickly
    ☹︎ Customer waiting time increased while staff completed paperwork
    ☹︎ Digital solutions previously failed because they ignored stylists’ working habits
    The challenge was not only digitisation, but respecting an established professional workflow.

    Design Goal

    ☺︎ Reduce documentation time without changing stylists’ mental model
    ☺︎ Maintain familiar interaction patterns from paper records
    ☺︎ Improve service efficiency and accuracy
    ☺︎ Shorten customer waiting time
    ☺︎ Enable fast data retrieval and automated calculations

    UX Strategy

    Instead of redesigning the workflow entirely, I adopted a “Paper → Enhanced Digital” approach:
    👉 Preserve what already works
    👉 Simplify repetitive tasks
    👉 Automate calculations invisibly

    Key design decisions

    ☺︎ Replicated the structure of existing paper forms to reduce learning cost
    ☺︎ Retained the drawing area used for perm rod planning (critical professional tool)
    ☺︎ Converted written descriptions into selectable checkboxes
    ☺︎ Automated transaction calculation based on selected services

    User Flow

    Customer Arrives → Open Daily Transactions Page → Search or Create Customer Record → Add New Transaction → Select Services (Auto Price Calculation) → View Customer History (if needed) → Save Transaction → Checkout Completed

    Compared with traditional paper records, the CRM system reorganizes the workflow around a Daily Transactions dashboard, which reflects the most important and frequently used task for hairdressers during daily operations. The Daily Transactions page serves as the system’s main index and operational hub. From this screen, hairdressers can:
    ☺︎ Add a new transaction at any time during or after a service
    ☺︎ Automatically calculate service costs based on selected items
    ☺︎ Monitor daily turnover and completed services in real time
    To improve efficiency in customer management, the system also enables quick record retrieval through multiple search methods, including:
    ☺︎ Customer name
    ☺︎ Phone number
    ☺︎ Birthday
    When accessing a customer’s profile, hairdressers can immediately view the member’s service and consumption history, allowing faster consultation, personalized recommendations, and smoother repeat visits.

    Outcome & Impact

    ☺︎ Reduced documentation time by 20%
    ☺︎ Shortened customer waiting time at checkout
    ☺︎ Reduced calculation errors
    ☺︎ Improved staff workflow satisfaction
    ☺︎ Faster access to customer history The redesign demonstrates how digitization succeeds when technology adapts to users — not the other way around.

    Cafe management website

    This project focuses on improving the internal ordering workflow for a small chain coffee shop. The goal was to streamline communication between branch staff and area managers by replacing fragmented manual communication with a centralized digital management platform.

    Problem

    The original ordering process relied heavily on manual communication through phone calls and group messaging. Area managers would randomly call each branch to check supply needs, while branch staff often submitted requests through group chats. This process created several operational challenges:
    ☹︎ Messages were frequently missed or overlooked
    ☹︎ Ordering requests required multiple follow-ups and confirmations
    ☹︎ Staff members often could not respond promptly due to peak service hours
    ☹︎ Order details were sometimes incomplete or inaccurate
    ☹︎ The lack of centralized tracking made prioritization difficult
    ☹︎ These inefficiencies increased communication costs and reduced staff availability for customer service

    Design Goal

    Create a centralized internal management platform that:
    ☺︎ Streamlines ordering communication between branches and management
    ☺︎ Improves task visibility and prioritization
    ☺︎ Reduces manual communication workload
    ☺︎ Provides easy access to operational resources for staff
    ☺︎ Enhances service efficiency by minimizing operational distractions

    Solution

    I designed a web-based management platform that allows branch staff to submit supply requests directly through a structured order system. The platform categorizes requests by urgency level and provides managers with a clear overview of ordering priorities. Beyond order management, the platform also serves as a centralized knowledge hub where staff can access company resources such as recipes, announcements, and training materials.

    UX Design Approach

    | Workflow Simplification |
    The interface was designed to reduce cognitive load by replacing informal communication with structured request submission. Clear input fields and predefined categories minimize ambiguity and reduce the need for follow-up clarification.

    | Task Visibility & Prioritization |
    Managers require fast overview access during busy operational periods. The dashboard design emphasizes visual hierarchy and urgency categorization to support rapid task sorting and decision-making.

    | Error Prevention & Accuracy |
    Including supplier details and standardized item naming helps reduce ordering mistakes and minimizes return or correction cycles.

    | Time-Saving Interaction Design |
    The platform reduces reliance on phone calls and messaging by consolidating requests into a single interface, allowing staff to submit orders asynchronously without interrupting service duties.

    | Information Accessibility |
    By integrating company resources into the platform, staff can quickly reference operational knowledge without switching tools or contacting management.

    Impact & Outcomes

    ☻ Reduced communication time and operational coordination costs
    ☻ Improved order tracking and transparency across branches
    ☻ Increased employee satisfaction by simplifying internal workflows
    ☻ Reduced service interruptions caused by manual communication
    ☻ Improved customer satisfaction by allowing staff to focus on in-store service rather than phone communication

    Matsukasa Bakery

    Overview

    Matsukasa is a neighborhood bakery founded by a professionally trained baker who graduated from Tokyo Seika Gakko. The bakery specializes in handcrafted baked goods inspired by Japanese wagashi craftsmanship. This project focused on strengthening the brand experience and improving customer recognition through a cohesive and culturally meaningful visual identity.

    Problem

    The bakery’s visual identity was inconsistent across customer touchpoints, including storefront signage, business cards, and packaging. This fragmented brand presentation reduced memorability and weakened Matsukasa’s ability to communicate its premium craftsmanship and cultural uniqueness. The lack of a unified system also limited scalability for future marketing and product packaging.

    Design Goal

    Develop a unified brand identity that reflects the bakery’s craftsmanship while enhancing customer trust and recognition across all interaction points.

    Solution

    I designed a Corporate Identity System (CIS) that standardized visual elements across physical and marketing materials. The new system created consistency across signage, print materials, and packaging, ensuring customers experience a clear and cohesive brand identity at every touchpoint.

    Design Approach

    The visual direction focused on expressing warmth, craftsmanship, and authenticity to reflect the bakery’s handcrafted philosophy and neighborhood presence. The owner wished to retain the original pinecone motif, which serves as an important brand symbol, while evolving the overall identity into a brighter and more welcoming visual experience. Japanese cultural elements were thoughtfully incorporated into the brand identity to differentiate Matsukasa within the competitive bakery market. These elements help communicate the bakery’s heritage, expertise, and dedication to traditional techniques. To reinforce an approachable neighborhood atmosphere, the bakery name was redesigned in a naïve and charming typographic style. This design choice creates a friendly and accessible personality that resonates with local customers and reflects the bakery’s community-focused positioning. The packaging design further strengthens cultural storytelling through the use of the phrase “HO JA,” written in Japanese characters. The phrase shares the same pronunciation as the Taiwanese word for “delicious,” creating a playful cross-cultural connection that enhances brand memorability and customer engagement. Design elements were simplified and standardized to improve readability, visual hierarchy, and consistency across applications. This approach ensures a seamless brand experience across all customer interaction points, from storefront signage to takeaway packaging.

    Impact

    ☻ Strengthened brand consistency across all customer touchpoints
    ☻ Improved brand recognition and customer trust
    ☻ Established a scalable identity system supporting future packaging and marketing expansion
    ☻ Enhanced communication of Matsukasa’s craftsmanship and cultural positioning
